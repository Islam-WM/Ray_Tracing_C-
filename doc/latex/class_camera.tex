\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
The Class \mbox{\hyperlink{class_camera}{Camera}} provides the functionality of the screen. 

The \mbox{\hyperlink{class_camera}{Camera}} consists of a screen and a vanishing point. The screen is a set of pixels that have some location in the scene. The \mbox{\hyperlink{class_camera}{Camera}} sends Rays trough every pixel. The vanishing point is the point that all of these rays originate from.

Since \mbox{\hyperlink{class_camera}{Camera}} is a child class of Object, it has a location, rotation and scale. The vanishing point is stored in the location parameter. The rotation is the rotation of the camera and scale consists of the x and y scale of the screen in the scene and the z-\/value of scale correndponds to the depth of the camera (the distance between screen and vanishing point). \begin{DoxySeeAlso}{See also}
\mbox{\hyperlink{class_scene___element}{Scene\+\_\+\+Element}}
\end{DoxySeeAlso}
\begin{DoxyWarning}{Warning}
One needs to be careful with how he chooses the x and y scale and resolution of the camera, because otherwise one will and up with a stretched image. It is advised to follow this rule\+: scale\mbox{[}0\mbox{]}/screen\+\_\+res\mbox{[}0\mbox{]} = scale\mbox{[}1\mbox{]}/screen\+\_\+res\mbox{[}1\mbox{]} 
\end{DoxyWarning}
\begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
Another way to store the resolution would be to store a dpi and the scale of the screen or to store the dpi and the resolution of the image. We decided to use the resolution and the scale of the screen, as we felt this gives us more control and is easier to work with since both the scale and dpi are values, that don\textquotesingle{}t provide very good intuition for the outcome of the render. 
\end{DoxyNote}

\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{screen\_points}{screen\_points}}
{\footnotesize\ttfamily vector$<$Vector3d$>$ Camera\+::screen\+\_\+points}



The vector stores the locations of the pixels in the global space. 

We need these points to compute the direction of the rays that are send from the vanishing point of the camera.

The location of the pixels depends on the resolution and the scale of the camera and is computed by the \mbox{\hyperlink{class_camera_a8e09889121bbb9f41ef5cb7eed9a5f4f}{compute\+\_\+screen\+\_\+points()}} function.
